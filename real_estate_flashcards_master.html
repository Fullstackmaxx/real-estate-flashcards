Q: What is an express contract?

A: A contract where terms are clearly stated in words, written or spoken.
Q: What is an implied contract?

A: A contract formed by actions rather than explicit words.
Q: Define bilateral contract.

A: A contract where both parties make promises to each other.
Q: What is consideration in a contract?

A: Something of value exchanged by both parties to create binding obligations.
Q: What contracts must be in writing under the Statute of Frauds?

A: Real estate contracts, agreements >1 year, debt guarantees, and more.
Q: What is the Parol Evidence Rule?

A: Written contract terms override prior verbal agreements unless clarifying.
Q: How can a contract be discharged?

A: Through performance, rescission, breach, novation, or agreement.
Q: What can a non-breaching party do?

A: Seek rescission, damages, or specific performance through legal means.
